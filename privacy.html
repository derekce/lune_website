Privacy Policy

Luna: Your Cosmic Companion

Last updated: June 13, 2026

The short version

Luna does not collect, store, or sell your personal information. There are no accounts, no analytics, no advertising, and no third-party trackers. Almost everything the app does happens entirely on your device. The one exception is weather: if you allow location access, your coordinates are sent to a weather service to look up current conditions, and nothing more.

Who we are

Luna ("the app", "we", "us") is an independent app created by DEKAI (Derek Page). This policy explains how the app handles information when you use it.

Information we do not collect

We want to be direct about this, because it's unusual: Luna has no user accounts and no backend server that stores your data. We do not collect your name, email address, contacts, photos, advertising identifiers, or analytics about how you use the app. We do not track you across apps or websites, and we do not sell or share data with data brokers.

Information that stays on your device

Some information is created and stored locally on your device so the app can work. It never leaves your device and we cannot see it:

  • Moon phase data — calculated locally on your device using astronomical formulas. No network connection is needed for this.
  • Your intentions and preferences — anything you write or set in the app, along with settings like sound toggles, are saved in your device's local storage.
  • Notification schedule — reminders are scheduled locally by your device's operating system.

Permissions the app may request

Luna only asks for a permission at the moment a feature needs it, and every feature works the same way whether you grant it or not (you simply won't see that specific feature). You can change or revoke any permission at any time in your device's Settings.

  • Location — used only to show local weather alongside the moon. If you allow it, your approximate or precise coordinates are sent to Open-Meteo, a free weather service, solely to retrieve current conditions for that location. We do not store your location, and it is not linked to your identity. Open-Meteo's handling of requests is governed by their own terms and privacy information.
  • Calendar — used only if you choose to save moon events (such as full moons) to your calendar. The app writes those events to the calendar you select on your device. Calendar contents are not transmitted to us.
  • Notifications — used to deliver moon-cycle and intention reminders. These are scheduled and shown locally by your device.

Third-party services

The app uses exactly one third-party service: Open-Meteo, for weather. A weather request includes only the geographic coordinates needed to return conditions for that spot. No identifiers about you or your device are attached. This is the only situation in which any data leaves your device.

Children's privacy

Luna is suitable for a general audience and does not knowingly collect personal information from anyone, including children. Because the app collects no personal data, there is nothing for us to gather from users of any age.

Data retention and deletion

Because we hold no personal data on any server, there is nothing for us to retain or delete on our end. Data stored locally on your device is removed when you delete the app, and you can clear saved content within the app or via your device settings at any time.

Changes to this policy

If the app's data practices change, we'll update this page and revise the "Last updated" date above. Material changes will be reflected here before they take effect.
